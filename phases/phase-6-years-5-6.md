#### Critical Reading Techniques
1. **Author's Purpose** â€” Why was this written? To persuade, inform, entertain, or explain?
2. **Bias Detection** â€” Does the author have a particular viewpoint? How can you tell?
3. **Tone Analysis** â€” What tone does the author use? Formal, humorous, sarcastic, sympathetic?

5. **Text Structure** â€” Cause/effect, compare/contrast, chronological, problem/solution

## âœï¸ Writing Component

### This is NEW compared to the OC test

The Selective test includes a 30-minute writing task. Students respond to a prompt (stimulus) and may write either a **narrative** or **persuasive** piece.

### Narrative Writing Framework

```
OPENING (Hook the reader)
  â†’ Engaging first sentence
  â†’ Establish character, setting, mood

COMPLICATION (Build tension)
  â†’ Problem or conflict introduced
  â†’ Rising action â€” things get worse

CLIMAX (Turning point)
  â†’ Most intense moment
  â†’ Character makes a key decision

RESOLUTION (Wrap up)
  â†’ Problem resolved
  â†’ Character has changed/learned something

CODA (Final punch)
  â†’ Reflection or twist
  â†’ Memorable final line
```

### Persuasive Writing Framework (PEEL Ã— 3)

```
INTRODUCTION
  â†’ Hook, context, state your position (thesis)

BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Strongest argument)
  â†’ Point: State your argument
  â†’ Evidence: Facts, statistics, examples
  â†’ Explain: Why this evidence supports your point
  â†’ Link: Connect back to your thesis

BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Second argument)
  â†’ Same PEEL structure

BODY PARAGRAPH 3 (Third argument or counter-argument)
  â†’ Address the opposing view, then refute it

CONCLUSION
  â†’ Restate position
  â†’ Summarise key arguments
  â†’ Call to action or thought-provoking final statement
```
